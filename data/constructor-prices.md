## Price Structure Analysis

The top 3 constructors (Mercedes, McLaren, Red Bull) are all $28M+ — extremely expensive. Ferrari at $23.3M is the clear value premium pick ($5-6M cheaper than the other Big 4).

Budget constructors are ALL under $12.5M. Haas at $7.4M is the consensus value pick (33% ownership, best-of-rest FP2 pace).

### Tier B Value Growth Targets
All constructors under $18.5M can gain +$0.6M per race:
- **Haas ($7.4M)** — needs just 7.4 × 0.40 = ~3.0 PPM for max gain. Trivially easy with Ocon/Bearman both scoring.
- **Racing Bulls ($6.3M)** — needs ~2.5 PPM. Lindblad's FP pace makes this achievable.
- **Cadillac ($6.0M)** — needs ~2.4 PPM. Risky (new team) but floor price.
- **Audi ($6.6M)** — needs ~2.6 PPM. Hulkenberg consistency helps.

### Constructor Budget Math
With Haas at $7.4M (not $13.0M as initially estimated), the constructor budget is much cheaper:
- Ferrari + Haas = **$30.7M** (leaves $69.3M for drivers)
- Ferrari + Racing Bulls = **$29.6M** (leaves $70.4M)
- Ferrari + Audi = **$29.9M** (leaves $70.1M)

This is $5-6M cheaper than our initial estimates and opens up a second premium driver.

### Ownership Insights
- **Ferrari (41%)** — most-owned constructor. Consensus premium pick.
- **Haas (33%)** — consensus value pick.
- **McLaren (17%)** and **Red Bull (10%)** — low ownership at their price points.
- **Aston Martin (3%)** — nobody wants them (Honda crisis).
