## 2026 Regulation Context

This is the first race under the biggest regulation overhaul since 2014. Active aero (X-mode/Z-mode) replaces DRS, the power unit is now 50/50 ICE/electric, and three new driver tools (Overtake Mode, Boost Mode, Recharge Modes) change the overtaking dynamic entirely. **All historical data is provisional.** This race IS the data.

Key unknowns for Race 1:
- Will active aero increase or decrease overtaking at Melbourne?
- Which PU suppliers actually have race-distance reliability?
- How much do energy management differences between teams affect late-race performance?
- How volatile are the new ICE-only race starts (no MGU-K for first 50m)?

- **Honda crisis:** Aston Martin (Alonso, Stroll) avoided entirely. Honda PU has multiple failures in testing. Confirmed correct to avoid.
- **Regulation watch:** This race is the baseline. After the checkered flag, record:
  1. Total overtake count vs historical Melbourne average (~40-45)
  2. Which teams faded in the final stint (energy management signal)
  3. Any PU-related DNFs (reliability signal)
  4. First-lap position changes (race start volatility signal)
  5. Active aero effectiveness — did X-mode/Z-mode change the racing character?
  6. Boost mode usage patterns — which drivers deployed aggressively vs conservatively?
