# F1 Fantasy 2026 Constructor Evaluation

## Why Constructors Matter

Constructors provide the **most consistent scoring floor** on your team because they:
- Score the combined total of both drivers across all sessions
- Get exclusive pitstop bonuses (up to +20 per fast stop + +5 fastest pit)
- Earn Q2/Q3 qualification bonuses (+10 for both drivers in Q3)
- Are less volatile than individual drivers (one driver's bad race is offset by the other)

A top constructor with fast pitstops and both-in-Q3 can add **30-40 bonus points per weekend** beyond the combined driver scores.

## Evaluation Criteria

### 1. Both-Driver Strength (Most Important)
The combined scoring of both drivers determines 70%+ of constructor fantasy value.

| Rating | Meaning | Example |
|--------|---------|---------|
| Elite | Both drivers podium contenders | McLaren (Norris + Piastri) |
| Strong | Both consistently in top 8 | Ferrari (Leclerc + Hamilton) |
| Split | One strong + one weak | Red Bull (Verstappen elite, Hadjar unproven) |
| Weak | Neither driver reliably in points | Alpine, Cadillac |

**Rule: Never pick a "Split" constructor unless the elite driver is so dominant that they compensate.** Red Bull is the only exception because Verstappen's ceiling is that high.

### 2. Pitstop Performance
Historical pitstop speed by team (sub-2.5s stops = 5+ pts each):

| Tier | Teams | Avg Stop Time | Typical Bonus per Race |
|------|-------|---------------|----------------------|
| Elite | McLaren, Red Bull | 2.0-2.3s | 10-20 pts |
| Good | Ferrari, Mercedes | 2.2-2.5s | 5-10 pts |
| Average | Williams, Haas | 2.3-2.7s | 2-5 pts |
| Slow | Alpine, Audi, Cadillac | 2.5-3.0s | 0-2 pts |

McLaren and Red Bull have the fastest pit crews, consistently delivering sub-2.0s stops worth +20 pts each.

### 3. Q2/Q3 Consistency
The +10 bonus for both drivers in Q3 is massive and recurring:

| Constructor | Both in Q3 Rate (2025) | Expected Bonus |
|-------------|----------------------|---------------|
| McLaren | ~85% | +8.5/race avg |
| Ferrari | ~80% | +8.0/race avg |
| Mercedes | ~75% | +7.5/race avg |
| Red Bull | ~60% (Perez/Hadjar drag) | +6.0/race avg |
| Williams | ~40% | +4.0/race avg |
| Haas | ~35% | +3.5/race avg |

## 2026 Constructor Rankings (Opening Day)

### Tier 1: Premium Picks

#### McLaren ($25.0M) — **Best Overall Pick**
- Both drivers are WDC contenders (Norris, Piastri)
- Fastest pit crew on the grid
- Highest both-in-Q3 rate
- Reigning WCC champions
- **Downside:** Expensive. Limits budget for drivers.

#### Ferrari ($23.0M) — **Best Value Premium**
- Leclerc fastest in pre-season testing
- Hamilton adds veteran consistency
- Strong pitstops (improved 2025)
- Underpriced vs Mercedes ($2M cheaper)
- **Downside:** Hamilton's F1 form declining since 2021

#### Mercedes ($21.0M) — **High Ceiling, Risky Floor**
- Russell is betting favorite for WDC
- Antonelli is talented but inconsistent (rookie errors expected)
- Compression ratio loophole rumors could make the car dominant
- **Downside:** Antonelli DNFs would be painful at this price

### Tier 2: Balanced Picks

#### Red Bull ($19.0M) — **Verstappen Dependency**
- Verstappen alone carries 70%+ of constructor points
- Hadjar is unproven in the second seat (historically cursed position)
- Fast pit crew (top 2)
- **Downside:** Classic "split" constructor. Paying $19M for essentially one driver.

#### Williams ($17.0M) — **Overpriced?**
- P5 in 2025 WCC, strong testing
- Albon + Sainz is a solid both-driver combination
- But $17M is steep for their likely pace
- **Downside:** May not maintain P5 pace with 2026 regulation changes

### Tier 3: Value Picks

#### Haas ($13.0M) — **BEST VALUE CONSTRUCTOR**
- Ocon + Bearman: both scored 24+ PPM in 2025
- Led the grid in total overtakes in 2025
- Both-driver strength at a budget price
- Tier B pricing means +$0.6M max price gain per race
- Projected P5-P6 pace (upgrades incoming)
- **Best Phase 1 value constructor**

#### Audi ($11.0M)
- New manufacturer entry (ex-Sauber)
- Hulkenberg is consistent but limited ceiling
- Bortoleto is a year-2 prospect
- Risk/reward play. Unknown car pace.

#### Racing Bulls ($9.0M)
- Two young drivers (Lawson, Lindblad)
- Red Bull B-team, decent car
- Volatile but very cheap
- Possible Phase 1 ultra-budget option

#### Cadillac ($7.0M)
- 11th team debut. No pace data.
- Perez + Bottas = experienced but slow
- Could surprise or could be last
- **Only pick if $7M savings enables a must-have driver**

#### Alpine ($5.0M)
- Cheapest constructor
- Worst 2025 season
- Testing showed improvement but from a very low base
- Last resort budget option

## Recommended Opening Day Constructors

*Prices verified from game on Mar 6, 2026.*

### Phase 1 Pairing
**Ferrari ($23.3M) + Haas ($7.4M) = $30.7M**

- Ferrari: premium anchor with testing form, $5-6M cheaper than the other Big 4 constructors
- Haas: best value constructor at $7.4M, overtake kings, only needs ~3.0 PPM for max +$0.6M gain
- Leaves **$69.3M for 5 drivers** — enough for 2 premium drivers

### Alternative Pairings
- **Ferrari ($23.3M) + Racing Bulls ($6.3M) = $29.6M** — even cheaper, frees $70.4M for drivers. Lindblad's FP pace makes Racing Bulls viable.
- **Ferrari ($23.3M) + Audi ($6.6M) = $29.9M** — Hulkenberg consistency, but Audi PU risk
- **Mercedes ($29.3M) + Haas ($7.4M) = $36.7M** — FP2 dominant but Mercedes is $6M more than Ferrari. Leaves only $63.3M for drivers.

### AVOID
- **Aston Martin ($10.3M)** — Honda engine failures, planned early retirements. 3% ownership confirms nobody wants them.
- **Red Bull as a constructor ($28.2M)** — paying $28.2M for a split team where only Verstappen delivers reliably. Own Verstappen as a driver instead.
