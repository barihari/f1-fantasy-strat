# F1 Fantasy 2026 Chip Strategy

## Overview

6 chips total, each usable **once per season**. Only one chip per race weekend. Chips cannot be cancelled once activated.

## Pre-Season Chips (Available Immediately)

### 1. Wildcard
**What it does:** Unlimited free transfers for one race weekend. No -10 penalties.

**When to use:**
- After a major regulation change impacts the grid order (e.g., mid-season upgrade packages)
- When your team structure is fundamentally wrong (3+ players underperforming)
- Phase transition (Races 10-14) when upgrading from value picks to premium structure

**Best race profile:** After a major shakeup, NOT at the first race. Wait until you have data showing a structural shift.

**2025 Top 500 data:** Most commonly used Races 6-10 during mid-season restructure.

---

### 2. 2X Boost (Team-Wide)
*Note: You already have a free 2X Boost on one driver every week. This chip is different — it's the standard weekly boost, not a special chip. The special boost chips are the 3X Boost (see below).*

The weekly 2X Boost is always active. Assign it to:
- Your highest-ceiling driver for the specific circuit
- At Sprint weekends, the boost covers all 3 sessions (Qualifying + Sprint + Race)
- At standard weekends, it covers Qualifying + Race

---

### 3. No Negative
**What it does:** All negative scores from your team are converted to zero for that weekend.

**When to use:**
- High-attrition races with wet weather risk (rain = DNFs)
- Circuits with high DNF history (Monaco, Baku, Singapore)
- When you suspect your value picks may DNF but want to keep them for price growth

**Best race profile:** Street circuits, wet races, chaotic weekends.

**Strategic timing:** Use early-mid season (Races 4-8) when your team has more volatile value picks who are DNF risks.

---

## Post-Race 1 Chips (Unlock After Completing Race 1)

### 4. 3X Boost
**What it does:** Your 2X boosted driver gets 3X instead. Triple their entire weekend score.

**When to use:**
- **Sprint weekends ONLY** — 3 scoring sessions means 3X multiplication across more events
- On a circuit where your boosted driver historically dominates
- When you're confident your premium driver will have a massive weekend

**Best race profile:** Sprint + high-overtake circuit where frontrunners dominate.

**2025 Top 500 data:** 54% used it at Qatar Sprint. Other popular targets were São Paulo Sprint and US GP Sprint.

**2026 targets (priority order):**
1. **Netherlands (R12)** — Sprint + high overtakes + Phase 2 budget
2. **Singapore (R16)** — Final Sprint, but street circuit (lower overtakes)
3. **Great Britain (R9)** — Sprint, but medium overtakes
4. **China (R2)** — Sprint, but too early (limited intel on driver form)
5. **Miami (R4)** — Sprint + high overtakes, but early season
6. **Canada (R5)** — Sprint + medium-high overtakes

---

### 5. Limitless
**What it does:** Budget cap removed for one race. Pick any 5 drivers + 2 constructors regardless of price.

**When to use:**
- Low-overtake circuits where grid position = race result (qualifying accuracy is highest)
- When premium drivers have the biggest edge and budget caps hold you back

**Best race profile:** Low-overtake tracks where P1/P2 qualifiers finish P1/P2.

**2025 Top 500 data:** 48.6% used it at Monaco. Also popular at Hungary.

**2026 targets (priority order):**
1. **Monaco (R6)** — Lowest overtakes on calendar. Qualifying IS the race.
2. **Hungary (R11)** — Low-medium overtakes, clean air dominant
3. **Azerbaijan (R15)** — Street circuit, low overtakes, but chaotic (consider No Negative pairing conflict)

---

### 6. Final Fix
**What it does:** After the first scoring session (Qualifying or Sprint Qualifying), you can swap one driver before the next session.

**When to use:**
- When a key driver has a qualifying disaster (crash, mechanical, penalty)
- As a safety net for uncertain races
- Pairs well with risky picks — take a chance knowing you can course-correct

**Best race profile:** Any race where you're taking a calculated risk on a volatile driver.

**2025 Top 500 data:** 49.2% used it at Azerbaijan (Baku). Street circuits with crash risk are ideal.

**2026 targets:**
1. **Azerbaijan (R15)** — Chaotic street circuit, crash risk, qualifying accidents common
2. **Singapore (R16)** — If not using 3X here, Final Fix is strong at a street circuit

## Decision Framework

Before each race, ask:
1. Is this a Sprint weekend? → Consider 3X Boost
2. Is this a low-overtake circuit? → Consider Limitless
3. Is there high DNF/crash risk? → Consider No Negative or Final Fix
4. Has the grid order fundamentally shifted? → Consider Wildcard
5. Is this race at geopolitical risk? → Do NOT use a chip here unless confirmed

**Default: SAVE the chip.** Only deploy when the match between chip and race profile is strong. Top players average 1 chip per 4 races.
