# F1 Fantasy 2026 Transfer Management

## Transfer Rules

| Rule | Details |
|------|---------|
| Free transfers per race | 2 |
| Carry-over | 1 unused free transfer carries to next race |
| Maximum banked | 3 (2 free + 1 carried) |
| Penalty for extra transfers | -10 points each |
| Net change rule (2026) | Transfers counted by net difference from previous lineup |

## The Net Change Rule (New for 2026)

Previously, every individual swap counted as a transfer. Now, only the **net difference** between your old lineup and new lineup counts.

**Example:** If you swap Driver A → Driver B, then later swap Driver B → Driver A before lock, that counts as **0 transfers** (net: no change). This gives more flexibility to experiment before lock.

**Strategic implication:** You can "try out" different lineups during the week and only finalize before lock. If you change your mind, swap back at no cost.

## Transfer Banking Strategy

### Phase 1 (Races 1-10): Bank Aggressively
- Use **0-1 transfers per race** in Phase 1
- Bank transfers for the transition period
- Only transfer if:
  - A driver is injured/dropped
  - A value pick has clearly lost pace (3+ races of declining PPM)
  - A specific circuit demands a specialist (e.g., Monaco → qualifiers)

### Transition (Races 10-14): Spend Banked Transfers
- Use banked transfers to restructure from value picks to premium assets
- Deploy Wildcard if 3+ changes needed
- Plan the restructure 2-3 races in advance

### Phase 2 (Races 14-24): Tactical Transfers
- Use transfers reactively based on weekly form
- Chase hot streaks (driver who just got an upgrade package)
- Protect the lineup for chip weekends

## When to Accept the -10 Penalty

Almost **never**. The -10 penalty is equivalent to losing a P10 finish. Only accept if:

1. **Protecting a chip weekend** — If deploying 3X Boost and a key driver is confirmed out (injury, grid penalty), swapping avoids a potential -60 effective loss (3X × -20 DNF)
2. **Massive price swing** — If NOT swapping means losing $0.6M and the incoming driver gains $0.6M, the $1.2M value difference may justify -10 pts (rare)
3. **Season-ending injury** — A driver's season is over and you need their slot

Alex Pearson (P3 Global 2025) took only **2 penalty hits** all season. That discipline is a competitive edge.

## Multi-Race Transfer Planning

### The "2-3 Race Block" Method
Plan transfers in blocks, not individually:

1. **Before each block:** Look at the next 2-3 races' circuit profiles
2. **Identify overlapping needs:** Which drivers suit all 2-3 circuits?
3. **Make transfers that work for the whole block,** not just the next race
4. **Avoid churn:** Swapping a driver in for one race and back out the next wastes transfer budget

### Example Block: Races 8-10 (Monaco → Barcelona → Austria)
- Monaco needs qualifiers (low overtakes)
- Barcelona/Austria need overtakers (high overtakes)
- **Solution:** Keep overtake-strong drivers (they still qualify decently) and use the 2X Boost on your best qualifier at Monaco, then shift boost to an overtaker at Barcelona/Austria

## Transfer Decision Matrix

| Scenario | Action |
|----------|--------|
| Driver underperformed for 1 race | HOLD — single race is noise |
| Driver underperformed for 3 races | TRANSFER — trend confirmed |
| Driver has grid penalty next race | EVALUATE — penalties create overtake upside |
| Driver's price about to drop $0.6M | TRANSFER — if replacement gains value |
| Driver's constructor got an upgrade | HOLD — they'll improve |
| Sprint weekend next, driver is Sprint-weak | TRANSFER — only if you have a free transfer |
| Chip weekend (3X/Limitless) and driver is questionable | TRANSFER — protect your chip investment |

## FP1/FP2 Scouting

Before finalizing transfers:
1. Watch FP1 and FP2 pace data
2. Check for:
   - Surprising pace from value picks (upgrade package?)
   - Mechanical issues for your drivers (reliability concern?)
   - Weather forecast changes (wet race favors certain drivers)
3. Make final transfer decisions after FP2, before lock
4. The Final Fix chip gives one more adjustment window after qualifying
