## Qualifying (Constructors)

Constructors score the **combined total** of both drivers plus:

| Achievement | Points |
|-------------|--------|
| Neither driver reaches Q2 | -1 |
| One driver reaches Q2 | +1 |
| Both drivers reach Q2 | +3 |
| One driver reaches Q3 | +5 |
| Both drivers reach Q3 | **+10** |
| Disqualified driver | -5 per driver |

### Race Bonuses
| Category | Points |
|----------|--------|
| Positions gained | +1 per position |
| Positions lost | -1 per position |
| Overtakes made | +1 per overtake |
| Fastest Lap | +10 |
| Driver of the Day | +10 (driver only, not constructor) |

Positions gained/lost are calculated from starting position to finishing position. Unclassified drivers get the -20 penalty instead of position loss. Cars starting from the pit lane are considered to have started from a position relative to the last car on the grid.

Overtakes: legally passing another on track; the passed driver must not be entering/in the pit lane, suffering car failure, or going unreasonably slow.

Constructors score the combined total of both drivers (excluding Driver of the Day) plus:

## Key Scoring Insights for Strategy

1. **Overtakes are massive** — Hulkenberg got 72% of his 2025 season points from overtakes alone (106 of 147)
2. **DNFs are devastating** — -20 in race, -10 in sprint. On a 2X boosted driver that's -40/-20 effective
3. **Pitstop bonuses are free points** — McLaren/Red Bull consistently sub-2.5s. A sub-2.0s stop is worth 20 pts
4. **Both-in-Q3 bonus (+10)** makes constructors with two strong qualifiers especially valuable
5. **Fastest Lap (+10) and DOTD (+10)** are bonus hunting grounds for elite drivers on dominant weekends
6. **Position gains from back of grid** can be huge — Verstappen scored 77 pts in Brazil 2024 starting P17
