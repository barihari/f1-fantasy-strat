Based on analysis of 2025 Top 500 Global League players, particularly the "Alex Pearson P3 Blueprint."

## The Alex Pearson Blueprint (P3 Global League 2025)

### Core Philosophy
"Build value first, score points second. The budget you build in Phase 1 IS your Phase 2 strategy."

### Key Principles

#### 1. Value-First Team Selection (Phase 1)
- Start with **1-2 premium anchors** and fill the rest with Tier B value picks
- Target drivers and constructors who will **consistently exceed their PPM threshold** for +$0.6M gains
- Accept scoring less than optimal in Races 1-10 to build toward a $120M+ budget
- Never chase short-term points at the cost of long-term value

#### 2. The "4A Structure" End Goal
- By Race 14, own **4 Tier A assets** ($20M+) — the four best-scoring drivers/constructors on the grid
- The 5th driver is a cheap placeholder who enables the budget math
- Both constructors should be strong (both-in-Q3 capable)
- This structure maximizes Phase 2 weekly scoring ceiling

#### 3. Differential Selection
- Don't just pick the consensus. Find drivers who are **underowned** but **over-performing**
- In 2025, Pearson picked Bearman when ownership was <5% — he scored 206 pts at PPM 27.84
- Low ownership + high scoring = you gain ranking while others don't
- Check ownership % on f1fantasytracker.com before finalizing picks

#### 4. Constructor Rotation Based on Both-Driver Strength
- Rotate constructors based on circuit suitability, not brand loyalty
- Prioritize constructors where **both drivers consistently reach Q3**
- The +10 bonus for both-in-Q3 stacks with pitstop bonuses to create 30+ constructor bonus points
- Avoid one-driver-carry constructors (e.g., Red Bull with only Verstappen delivering)

#### 5. Transfer Discipline
- Pearson took only **2 penalty hits (-10 pts each) all season**
- This means 22 of 24 races were managed within the free transfer allowance
- Bank transfers aggressively in Phase 1
- Plan transfers in 2-3 race blocks, not week-by-week

#### 6. FP1/FP2 Scouting Methodology
- Watch practice sessions for **long-run pace** (more predictive than qualifying sims)
- Look for: tire degradation, car balance, driver confidence
- Check for surprise pace from value picks (mid-season upgrades)
- Finalize lineup decisions only after FP2 data

#### 7. "Plan 2-3 Races Ahead" Transfer Philosophy
- Before making any transfer, ask: "Does this driver work for the next 2-3 circuits?"
- Avoid single-race specialists unless it's a chip weekend
- Build transfer plans that minimize total swaps while maximizing value across a block

## Strategy Anti-Patterns (What NOT to Do)

### 1. "Heart Picks"
Choosing drivers you personally like regardless of value. You manage ONE team — it must be purely competitive.

### 2. "Knee-Jerk Transfers"
Swapping a driver after one bad race. PPM is a 3-race rolling average; one bad race is noise.

### 3. "Set and Forget"
Not updating your 2X Boost, not checking practice data, not monitoring price changes. Active management wins.

### 4. "Chip Panic"
Deploying a chip because you haven't used one in a while, not because the race profile matches. Save > waste.

### 5. "Budget Tunnel Vision"
Ignoring value building in Phase 1 because "I want points now." The budget gap by Race 14 between a value-builder and a points-chaser is $15-20M — that's an extra premium driver.

### 6. "Ignoring Constructors"
Constructors are consistent, high-floor scorers with exclusive pitstop bonuses. They should get equal attention as drivers.
