# F1 Fantasy 2026 Season Phases

## The Two-Phase Strategy

The dominant strategy used by top players splits the season into two distinct phases with a transition period.

---

## Phase 1: Value Building (Races 1-10)

### Goal
Maximize team value growth by owning Tier B assets that gain +$0.6M per race.

### Target
Grow team value from $100M to **$120-130M** by Race 10.

### Team Structure
- **1-2 premium anchors** (Tier A) for baseline scoring + 2X Boost target
- **3-4 value drivers** (Tier B, $5.9M-$7.4M) that consistently score above their PPM threshold
- **1 premium constructor** (to capture pitstop bonuses)
- **1 value constructor** (Tier B, cheap enough to gain +$0.6M/race)

### Key Principles
1. **Points are secondary to value growth.** A driver scoring 10 pts at $6M is more valuable than one scoring 20 pts at $25M because the cheap driver gains +$0.6M while the expensive one only gains +$0.3M.
2. **Protect your value picks.** Don't transfer out a driver who's on a price-gain streak unless they're injured or team-swapped.
3. **Use Budget Builder weekly** to confirm your value picks will meet the PPM threshold.
4. **Save power chips.** Only Wildcard and No Negative are viable in Phase 1.

### Phase 1 Transfer Strategy
- Keep transfers minimal (bank them for Phase 2)
- Only transfer if a value pick is clearly broken (DNF streak, lost car pace)
- Watch for mid-season upgrades that boost cheap teams (Haas, Audi, Racing Bulls)

---

## Transition: The Upgrade Window (Races 10-14)

### Goal
Begin converting Tier B value picks into Tier A premium scorers.

### Trigger
Upgrade when:
- A Tier B driver's price has peaked (PPM trending downward, approaching natural ceiling)
- A Tier A driver has had a price dip and is now "on sale"
- Your total budget is $120M+ and can support 3-4 Tier A assets

### How to Upgrade
1. **Identify which value picks have maxed out.** A $7.4M driver who's risen to $11M may now be PPM-neutral.
2. **Sell high on value picks.** Transfer them out when they're at peak price.
3. **Buy Tier A on dips.** After a bad race, a premium driver may lose $0.3M — buy the dip.
4. **Wildcard is strongest here.** If you need 3+ changes to restructure, deploy the Wildcard.

### Target Post-Transition Structure
- 3-4 Tier A drivers (including your 2X Boost anchor)
- 1-2 remaining value picks (still gaining $0.6M/race)
- 1-2 premium constructors

---

## Phase 2: Points Maximization (Races 14-24)

### Goal
Score maximum weekly points with the strongest possible team.

### Target Structure: "4A"
- **4 Tier A drivers** ($20M+ each) as main scorers
- **1 budget driver** (cheapest available to make budget work)
- **2 strong constructors** (ideally both with both-in-Q3 capability)

### Key Principles
1. **Points are king.** Value growth no longer matters; only weekly scores count.
2. **Deploy power chips strategically.** 3X Boost, Limitless, and Final Fix are Phase 2 weapons.
3. **2X Boost always on your best driver.** In Phase 2, this should be Verstappen, Norris, or Piastri.
4. **Sprint weekends are super-scoring events.** Netherlands (R14) and Singapore (R18) are key.

### Phase 2 Transfer Strategy
- More aggressive transfers to chase form
- Use banked transfers from Phase 1 discipline
- Still avoid -10 penalties unless protecting a 3X Boost weekend

## Value Growth Projections

### Conservative Scenario
- 3 Tier B drivers gaining +$0.4M/race avg for 10 races = +$12.0M
- 2 Tier B constructors gaining +$0.3M/race avg for 10 races = +$6.0M
- Total: **+$18.0M** → $118M effective budget

### Aggressive Scenario
- 4 Tier B drivers gaining +$0.5M/race avg for 10 races = +$20.0M
- 1 Tier B constructor gaining +$0.5M/race for 10 races = +$5.0M
- Total: **+$25.0M** → $125M effective budget

### Target
Aim for the middle: **+$20-22M** by the end of Phase 1.
