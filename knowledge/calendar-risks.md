# F1 Fantasy 2026 Calendar Risk Assessment

## Geopolitical Risks: Middle East Races

Due to the ongoing Iran-Israel-USA conflict, two remaining Middle East championship rounds face potential cancellation or relocation.

**Bahrain and Saudi Arabia are no longer on the 2026 championship calendar** (confirmed per [formula1.com/en/racing/2026](https://www.formula1.com/en/racing/2026)). Bahrain hosts pre-season testing only.

### Active At-Risk Rounds

| Round | Race | Risk Level | Strategic Impact |
|-------|------|-----------|-----------------|
| R21 | Qatar GP (Lusail) | **MODERATE** | Late-season race. If cancelled, remaining chip deployment window shrinks. |
| R22 | Abu Dhabi GP (Yas Marina) | **MODERATE** | Season finale, elite overtakes. If cancelled, any unused chips are wasted. |

### Risk Assessment Factors
- Iran-Israel tensions have escalated since 2024
- USA involvement creates additional diplomatic complexity
- FIA/Liberty Media historically cautious about safety (cancelled Russia 2022, Bahrain 2011)
- Qatar and Abu Dhabi have strong multi-year contracts with significant financial motivation to keep racing
- Monitor formula1.com weekly for official announcements

### Historical Precedent
- **Russia 2022:** Cancelled after Ukraine invasion. Replaced with Portimao (Portugal).
- **China 2020-2023:** Cancelled due to COVID. Replaced with various European circuits.
- **Bahrain 2011:** Cancelled due to civil unrest. Not replaced that season.
- **Bahrain/Saudi 2026:** Not scheduled as championship rounds (Bahrain = testing only).

## Strategic Implications

### Rule 1: Never Anchor a Season-Defining Chip to an At-Risk Race
If you're planning to use 3X Boost at a specific race, that race must be confirmed safe. Moving a chip plan due to last-minute cancellation leads to suboptimal deployment.

### Rule 2: Maintain Chip Flexibility
For every chip, have a **primary target** and a **backup target** that is NOT at geopolitical risk:

| Chip | Primary Target | Backup (Non-Risk) |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| 3X Boost | Netherlands R12 (Sprint) | Great Britain R9 (Sprint) |
| Limitless | Monaco R6 | Hungary R11 |
| Final Fix | Azerbaijan R15 | Singapore R16 |
| No Negative | Situational (rain) | Canada R5, Brazil R19 |
| Wildcard | R4-R8 window | Any non-risk race |

Note: All primary targets are already at non-risk venues. The risk is mainly for:
- Players counting on Abu Dhabi (R22) as a final scoring blowout
- Late-season chip holders who need Qatar/Abu Dhabi as their last deployment window

### Rule 3: Front-Load Chip Usage If Risk Increases
If mid-season intelligence suggests Qatar/Abu Dhabi will be cancelled:
- Use remaining chips earlier in the European/Americas swing (R4-R20)
- Don't hold a chip waiting for R21/R22 if those races are uncertain
- The cost of an unused chip > the cost of slightly suboptimal deployment

### Rule 4: Value Implications of Replacement Circuits
If a Middle East race is replaced:
- The replacement will have **zero historical fantasy data**
- Treat replacement circuits as high-uncertainty (like Madrid R14)
- Conservative picks, no chip usage at replacement venues
- Historical replacements have been European circuits (Portimao, Imola, Istanbul) which tend to be medium-high overtake permanent circuits

## Monitoring Protocol

Check these sources before each recommendation:
- **formula1.com/en/racing/2026** — official calendar changes (already Step 4 in consultant rule)
- **FIA press releases** — safety assessments
- **Major news outlets** — geopolitical developments
- **F1 team travel announcements** — teams cancelling freight is a strong cancellation signal

If the calendar changes, follow the **Calendar change protocol** in `data/race-calendar.md` and update this file's round references accordingly.
