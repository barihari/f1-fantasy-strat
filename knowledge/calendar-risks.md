# F1 Fantasy 2026 Calendar Risk Assessment

## Geopolitical Risks: Middle East Races

Due to the ongoing Iran-Israel-USA conflict, several races in the Middle East face potential cancellation or relocation. This directly impacts chip planning and long-term strategy.

### At-Risk Races

| Round | Race | Risk Level | Strategic Impact |
|-------|------|-----------|-----------------|
| R4 | Bahrain GP (Sakhir) | **HIGH** | High-overtake circuit, good for value picks. If cancelled, Phase 1 loses a scoring opportunity. |
| R5 | Saudi Arabian GP (Jeddah) | **HIGH** | Low-overtake street circuit. Was a potential Limitless target. |
| R23 | Qatar GP (Lusail) | **MODERATE** | Late-season race. If cancelled, remaining chip deployment window shrinks. |
| R24 | Abu Dhabi GP (Yas Marina) | **MODERATE** | Season finale, elite overtakes. If cancelled, any unused chips are wasted. |

### Risk Assessment Factors
- Iran-Israel tensions have escalated since 2024
- USA involvement creates additional diplomatic complexity
- FIA/Liberty Media historically cautious about safety (cancelled Russia 2022)
- Some races have multi-year contracts that provide financial motivation to keep them
- Bahrain and Abu Dhabi are F1's most financially invested hosts

### Historical Precedent
- **Russia 2022:** Cancelled after Ukraine invasion. Replaced with Portimao (Portugal).
- **China 2020-2023:** Cancelled due to COVID. Replaced with various European circuits (Portimao, Imola, Turkey).
- **Bahrain 2011:** Cancelled due to civil unrest. Not replaced that season.

## Strategic Implications

### Rule 1: Never Anchor a Season-Defining Chip to an At-Risk Race
If you're planning to use 3X Boost at a specific race, that race must be confirmed safe. Moving a chip plan due to last-minute cancellation leads to suboptimal deployment.

### Rule 2: Maintain Chip Flexibility
For every chip, have a **primary target** and a **backup target** that is NOT at geopolitical risk:

| Chip | Primary Target | Backup (Non-Risk) |
|------|---------------|-------------------|
| 3X Boost | Netherlands R14 (Sprint) | Great Britain R11 (Sprint) |
| Limitless | Monaco R8 | Hungary R13 |
| Final Fix | Azerbaijan R17 | Singapore R18 |
| No Negative | Situational (rain) | Canada R7, Brazil R21 |
| Wildcard | R6-R10 window | Any non-risk race |

Note: All primary targets are already at non-risk venues. The risk is mainly for:
- Players who planned around Saudi Arabia (R5) for Limitless
- Players counting on Abu Dhabi (R24) as a final scoring blowout
- Late-season chip holders who need Qatar/Abu Dhabi as their last deployment window

### Rule 3: Front-Load Chip Usage If Risk Increases
If mid-season intelligence suggests Middle East races will be cancelled:
- Use remaining chips earlier in the European/Americas swing (R6-R21)
- Don't hold a chip waiting for Q23/R24 if those races are uncertain
- The cost of an unused chip > the cost of slightly suboptimal deployment

### Rule 4: Value Implications of Replacement Circuits
If a Middle East race is replaced:
- The replacement will have **zero historical fantasy data**
- Treat replacement circuits as high-uncertainty (like Madrid R16)
- Conservative picks, no chip usage at replacement venues
- Historical replacements have been European circuits (Portimao, Imola, Istanbul) which tend to be medium-high overtake permanent circuits
